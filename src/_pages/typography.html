    How your degree award is calculated

    The algorithms which apply to Ulster University awards, including courses delivered at partner institutions, were introduced from May 2019. The best outcome from previous and current algorithm calculations has been extended due to Covid 19 for 2020/21 enrolments.

    Your latest module marks, which may include resit marks, will apply within your final award calculations.

    Every student has the right to have the best outcome calculated from any of the degree algorithms that was in place during their period of enrolment. This includes gap years for placement or leave of absence.

    How the current algorithm is calculated

    Undergraduate (FT/PT)

    • 70% of final year (level 6)
    • 30% of your marks from the year before (level 5)

    Integrated Masters (FT/PT)

    • 50% of your final year (level 7)
    • 30% - of your marks from the year before (level 6)
    • 20% - of your marks in year 3 (level 5)

    How are awards calculated for each year?

    There are different calculations based on when you entered Ulster University.

    For students enrolled before 2020/21

    The best mark from either of these two calculations will be used for your degree classification:

    1. Previous award algorithm - 100% of final year marks (level 6)
    2. Current award algorithm - 70% of final year (level 6) and 30% of your marks from the year before (level 5)

    This calculation applies to:

    • Academic Year 19/20
      • Undergraduate - Year 3 (cohort 17/18)
      • Integrated Masters - Year 4 (cohort 16/17)
    • Academic Year 20/21
      • Undergraduate - Year 3 (cohort 18/19)
      • Integrated Masters - Year 4 (cohort 17/18)
    • Academic Year 21/22
      • Undergraduate - Year 3 (cohort 19/20)
      • Integrated Masters - Year 4 (cohort 18/19)
    • Academic Year 22/23
      • Integrated Masters -  Year 4 (cohort 19/20)

    For students enrolled after 2021/22

    Your degree will be calculated based on 70% of final year marks and 30% of you marks from the year before.

    This calculation applies for students who will be in Year 3 or above in the academic year 2022/2023.

    Award calculations for other courses

    Courses with approved variation of awards regulations (PSRB requirements)

    The current degree algorithm does not apply for students on these courses. Each course have specific regulations with award marks calculated manually under applicable algorithm. This also relates to students who completed Level 5 modules in 2020/21.

    Associate Awards DPP(I) / DIAS

    Sandwich / intercalary years are not considered in how your award is calculated discounted from calculation

    Students with Advanced Standing

    Student achieved 60 credit points or more at level 5 (20/21)

    The best mark from either of these two calculations will be used for your degree classification:

    1. Previous algorithm - 100% of final year marks
    2. Current algorithm - 70% of final year marks and 30% of your marks from the year before
    Student who have achieved less than 60 credit points at level 5, or enters Ulster University at level 6
    • Calculation is based on level 6 at Ulster University only

    Students will be individually notified of how their Integrated Masters course award is calculated.

    Foundation Degree Access

    There are three different calculations based on the dates and year you entered Ulster University.

    Students who entered final year in 2018/2019

    Your degree is calculated based on 100% of final year marks (level 6) at Ulster University only

    Year 2 between 2017/2018 to 2020/2021

    The best mark from either of these two calculations will be used for your degree classification:
  • Previous algorithm - 100% of final year marks
  • Current algorithm - 70% of final year marks and 30% of your marks from the year before

    • Students who progressed to Ulster University from 2019 onwards

    Your degree is calculated using the current algorithm - 70% of final year marks and 30% of your marks from the year before
