PG Certificate in Professional Development (Understanding International Business)

Do you have a responsibility and interest in pursuing business growth and strategy in organisations? Or do you have ambition to sell products and services into GB or into other overseas markets? This course is for you.

Award:

PG Certificate

Study level:

Postgraduate

Course delivery:

Fully Online

Mode:

Part-time

Duration:

12 weeks

About this course

Given the pace at which changes in work are unfolding, adapting to future skills needs is a necessity for companies who wish to succeed in the world of global business. The global pandemic has shown that we are inextricably connected whether we choose to be or not, in a post-Brexit world where the economic impacts of Covid-19 are large, disruptive and long term, international business skills are core to regional growth.

With a focus on export you will be made aware of the opportunities open to Northern Ireland companies both through the changing business environment post Covid-19 and also as the UK moves towards and through Brexit.

The course is aimed at those who have a responsibility and interest in pursuing business growth and strategy in organisations, or have ambition to sell products and services into GB or into other overseas markets.

You will be given the tools to evaluate an organisation’s growth opportunities within the context of a changed and changing global business environment.

The course is suitable for those who wish to work in a variety of industries in managerial or decision making roles and has a particular focus on pursuing business growth, selling into overseas markets.

In addition to the academic knowledge and skills acquired throughout the course, you will also gain experience in using online technology for groupwork, collaboration and presentation.

Delivery format (fully online)

The course is delivered online with much of the material available on demand.

You will be asked to attend some live sessions.

The course is assessed via coursework, there are no formal examinations.

You will complete individual as well as group projects which will be assessed.

In some modules you will be able to explore a business issue relevant to your own job or organisation allowing for a practical focus to learning.

In addition to the academic team you will also hear from industry experts and thought leaders which will help supplement your studies.

Modules

Context of Global Business

This module introduces you to the key concepts and theories in global business, and the contemporary global business environment.

The module will provide you with the necessary knowledge and skills to understand the nature of the global business environment and global business operations.

Importantly, through considering a range of both global large and small firms, and reflecting 'real world' activities, this module will assist you to take the first steps towards self-confidence with regard to global business and global citizenship.

It will examine the current ‘unique’ international business environment taking into account both current and future implications of Covid 19 and Brexit.

Strategic Digital Transformation

This module introduces you to the concepts that underpin business intelligence and data analytics as part of an enterprise-wide digital transformation strategy.

You will get the opportunity to create a data management strategy that will contain an evidence-based performance management dashboard to aid data manipulation and decision-making.

Data visualisation and reporting will allow you to understand the impact that data has for business recovery.

Global Strategy Development and Implementation

This module develops an understanding of the strategic management of an SME engaged in international marketing and trade. This includes understanding how the competitive position of a firm is devised based on analysing structure, country-based sources of advantage and their distinctive competencies.

Further, translating strategy into organisational action requires understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of the many organisational forms and processes that may be used to attain the desired competitive position. Many inner-organisational and outer organisational factors affect the implementation of the global strategy, therefore it is vital for participants to understand global strategy implementation success factors.

Entry requirements

The course is specifically targeted towards people who have been directly affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. To be eligible for a funded place on the course, you must meet one of the criteria below:

  • Unemployed since 1 March 2020 – evidence: redundancy notice or recent welfare benefit
  • Furloughed – evidence: provide confirmation from employer
  • Economically Inactive and partner furloughed, unemployed or on reduced earnings as a result of impact of COVID-19 – evidence: confirmation from employer; benefit/grant application/HMRC scheme application
  • Self-employed and income affected by COVID-19 – evidence: benefit or Self Employed Income Support application

    You must also meet the following entry criteria:

  • You hold a Second-Class Honours Degree or better
  • OR have significant managerial / business experience
