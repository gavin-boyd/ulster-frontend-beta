NINE DTP Scholarship: Fairness, Accountability & Transparency in Artificial Intelligence for Social Good

This project is funded by: Funder

Subject: Subject title

Summary

Ulster University is offering a 3-year PhD scholarship through Northern Ireland and North East Doctoral Training Partnership (NINE DTP) on ‘Artificial Intelligence for Social Good’. Successful candidates should be able to begin this studentship by April 1 2019. The uptake from the field of Artificial Intelligence (AI) is growing exponentially. But AI raises ethical, social and humanitarian challenges. The focus of this PhD will be in understanding the implications of ‘Algorithmic bias’, e.g., when algorithms perpetuate human biases due to the use of biased human curated datasets that are used to train the machine learning algorithms.

Essential criteria

  • Upper Second Class Honours (2:1) Degree or equivalent from a UK institution (or overseas award deemed to be equivalent via UK NARIC)
  • Research proposal of 2000 words detailing aims, objectives, milestones and methodology of the project

If the University receives a large number of applicants for the project, the following desirable criteria may be applied to shortlist applicants for interview.
