Take 5 Ulster University

FIVE steps to improving your wellbeing and getting more involved in student life.

The Take 5 campaign is designed to help you get fully involved in university life, while also encouraging you to achieve your personal and academic goals. Each week focuses on a different theme to support your wellbeing.

  • Connect

      Week commencing 16 September.

    Welcome to Ulster University! Your adventure starts here.

    Reach out, ask questions and get involved. There is an exciting mix of events this week across all campuses where you can meet other students, chat to staff and discover the range of support services on hand to help you get settled into university life. Start to make those all-important connections now.

    • ulster.ac.uk/welcome
  • Be Active

      Week commencing 23 September.

    Be active in your UU community by joining a club, society or the Student Council.

    Get involved in one of our sports clubs or try a new physical activity. Students can take advantage of new term fitness offers including 20% off 12-month gold memberships at your Sports Centre. The team will be on campus this week for more details and to help you join up.

    As an Ulster student, you are automatically a member of the Students’ Union. With over 90 different societies to choose from, there is something for everyone, whatever your interest.

    Alternatively, have your voice heard and help shape the student experience by joining the Student Council. Chat to the UUSU team for more information, and remember to check out the Freshers’ Fair.

    • ulster.ac.uk/sport
    • uusu.org
  • Keep Learning

      Week commencing 30 September.

    This week is all about learning. Explore the new collaborative learning spaces in your campus library this week. IT support clinics will also be running if you have any IT issues or questions.

    Don’t be afraid to try something new or rediscover a lost hobby. If you haven’t already, chat with UUSU about the range of clubs and societies on offer and get involved.

    • ulster.ac.uk/library
    • ulster.ac.uk/isd/students
    • uusu.org
  • Take Notice

      Week commencing 7 October.

    Hopefully you are starting to settle into university life, or getting back into routine if you are a returning student. This week we encourage you to stop, pause or take a moment to look around you.

    With ‘World Mental Health Day’ on 10 October, there will be lots of activity on campus across the week focusing on our student-led mental health campaign, ‘Mind Your Mood’. This initiative helps students to understand and improve their mental health, while breaking down any stigma.

    Your student experience extends both on and off campus. Be a good neighbour, look out for your friends, and be aware of your personal safety.

    • ulster.ac.uk/wellbeing
    • ulster.ac.uk/about/profile/community-relations
  • Give

      Week commencing 14 October.

    This week is all about giving. Do something nice for a friend, family member or stranger.

    Chat to the UUSU team about the range of volunteering opportunities available and how you can give your time to help others. Volunteering can also count towards the Ulster EDGE Award, which provides official recognition of relevant activities undertaken outside of your studies.

    • uusu.org/get_involved/volunteering
    • ulster.ac.uk/careers
